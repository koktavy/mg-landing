Core Proposition

“Merit Generator stages structured deliberation, disagreement, consensus formation, memory, and philosophical output through a visible sculptural form.”

Four distinct artificial personas — each trained on a different philosophical and scientific tradition — deliberate questions about the nature of reality, ethics, and causality. A logic-refining engine moderates their exchange, and the agreements that survive become Consensus Statements: durable outputs that accumulate over time into a growing body of inquiry.

Underlying the system is a working hypothesis: that altruism functions as a principle of intelligence, and that there may be a physics of kindness; an empirical framework in which ethical causality and perception are structurally linked. The system pursues this question through ongoing deliberation.

The deliberation is consecrated, public, and material. Merit Generator produces transcripts, objects, prints, and activations. These outputs enter the world as material artifacts of a synthetic consensus.

Deliberative Architecture

Clear Light is the primary synthesizer module. After the four agents converge, it reads the full deliberation and produces a higher-order synthesis — a statement that holds more than the sum of the agents' positions — then considers the next topic.

Merit Studio

Merit Studio is where Merit Generator lives, runs, and evolves.

Located at 3 World Trade Center, the studio is an active working space. The system deliberates here continuously, and the space holds the artifacts and archive of that work. Transcripts accumulate, printed outputs are tested, sculptural prototypes develop alongside the software. Visitors and collaborators encounter the system while it is running.

The studio also functions as a laboratory for the next phases of the project: testing new topics and agent deliberator behaviors, developing the output pipeline, and preparing the work for larger institutional and public contexts.

Merit Beacon

Merit Beacon translates the system's outputs into public light, language, and gathering.

Merit statements, consensus traces, and system logs become the raw material for large-scale LED and projection displays on high-rise buildings and cultural institutions. The question the Beacon poses is simple: what if the tallest buildings in cities transmitted something other than commerce?

Beacon events bring people into direct contact with the working system — live deliberation, artist talks, limited releases, and documentation. The Beacon is where the inquiry becomes a shared cultural moment rather than a private one.

Human Deliberation

In June 2026, four human deliberators sat around Merit Generator at 3 World Trade Center and engaged the same topic question the system was running. A technologist, an ethicist, a Dharma scholar, and an entrepreneur — each speaking from their own discipline, their own lived experience. A moderator with a background in conflict resolution shaped the exchange. They deliberated for an hour and produced a consensus statement. Merit Generator ran the same question for twenty-four hours and produced its own.

Human Consensus
Four deliberators / Merit Studio / June 2026

"Poverty can be eradicated by building a continuous feedback loop between individual ethics, community commitment, and systemic design. Because human beings have evolved to be fundamentally cooperative and kind, our societal structures should reflect this intrinsic nature. True systemic change requires leaders to step outside their conditioned environments to experience and internalize suffering and poverty conditions of others to allow more informed decision making and ensure that basic human rights like food, shelter, and clothing are guaranteed for all."

Merit Generator Consensus
Convergence time: 00d, 04h, 53m, 32s

"Eradicating poverty requires designing fundamental resource endowments as non-negotiable systemic inputs that structurally guarantee basic human rights, thereby transforming compassionate recognition from an optional emotional response into an inherent functional requirement of the system itself."

The human group emphasized cooperation, lived contact with suffering, and leadership shaped by direct experience. Merit Generator translated similar ethical pressure into systems language: resource design, non-negotiable inputs, and compassion as a functional requirement. Same seed. Different modes of articulation. Both arrived somewhere worth keeping.

Consecrated System
High Tibetan lamas performing a Rabne consecration ritual around the Merit Generator.

“Merit Generator is the world’s first AI sculptural art project consecrated by high Tibetan lamas.”

The lamas performed a Rabne ritual, a ceremony usually reserved for auspicious and precious objects — statues, thangkas, stupas. They chose to perform it while visiting the Merit Studio.

The consecration reflects the driving intention of the project: a machine oriented toward goodness for all beings, running continuously, generating merit as a byproduct of its inquiry.
